Project Overview

What We Built

Residential solar panels in arid regions lose up to 25% output from dust accumulation. Our 4-person team designed a compact autonomous device that attaches to the panel and runs a sequential clean-and-dry cycle — restoring transmissibility with minimal water and power.

Water System

Servo-driven water tube with 8 flat-cone nozzles (110° spray) sweeping a 36.3° arc. Operates at 50–70 psi residential supply pressure with no pump required. Flow confirmed at 9 gal/min via digital flow meter. Nozzle count and sweep angle derived from first-principles pressure-diameter calculations.

Air Drying System

Six brushless EDF fans in pairs, sequenced via Arduino PWM for uniform panel coverage. Channels designed using Bernoulli's equation across 6 temperature conditions. Angular geometry redesigned from curved profile after iterative testing — eliminated warping with negligible velocity loss.

Engineering Work Performed

How It Was Built

Every subsystem designed from first principles, tested iteratively, and validated against quantified criteria.

Structural FEA

SolidWorks FEA of outer casing under fan thrust loads confirmed max deflection 1.509E-03 mm on electronics housing top plate — validating structural integrity at operating loads.

Air System Design

Applied Bernoulli's equation and continuity (T = pAe²/Aj × Ve²) across 6 temperature conditions to determine exit slit geometry and fan selection for ≥20 m/s drying velocity.

Electronics & Control

Programmed Arduino Uno to sequence 2 servo motors and 6 ESC-driven fans via PWM mapping. Staggered activation (left → right → middle) manages inrush current. ESC calibration ensures consistent startup across all units.

Iterative Design

Resolved PETG warping across 5 print cycles — identified excessive member thickness as root cause. Thinned members, increased screw attachment points, redesigned air channel to angular geometry. Velocity loss confirmed negligible via measured fan data.

MATLAB Validation

Wrote computePanelCleaning() using grayscale thresholding and morphological operations (imopen, imclose, bwareaopen) to quantify cleaned surface area from before/after images. Confirmed 94.59% coverage — exceeding the 90% target.
